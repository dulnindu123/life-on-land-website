💧 Water Cycling

Understanding how terrestrial ecosystems act as nature's ultimate filtration system.

Ecosystems play a vital role in the water cycle. Forests, for example, act like giant sponges, soaking up rainwater and slowly releasing it into streams and rivers. This helps prevent floods and ensures a steady supply of water during dry periods. Furthermore, vegetation and soil act as natural filters, removing pollutants and purifying water as it moves through the environment.

The Filtration Process

As water flows through soil and roots, impurities are trapped and broken down by microorganisms. This natural process provides us with clean drinking water and healthy aquatic habitats. Without healthy land ecosystems, this filtration system breaks down, leading to water scarcity and pollution.

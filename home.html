Life on Land

Life on land encompasses the rich tapestry of terrestrial ecosystems—from tropical rainforests and savannas to temperate woods, grasslands, deserts, and tundra. These environments host millions of plant and animal species, cycle nutrients through soils, regulate the climate, and provide food, water, and cultural value to human societies. Without healthy land ecosystems, we risk cascading impacts on agriculture, clean water supplies, climate stability, and cultural heritage.

  • Facts
  • Over 80% of the world's terrestrial species live in forests.
  • Almost 1 in 4 mammal species is threatened by habitat loss.
  • Soils store nearly 3x more carbon than the atmosphere.

The Importance of Land Ecosystems

Terrestrial ecosystems are the planet's living infrastructure. They regulate our climate, purify water, build and maintain soils, pollinate crops and furnish countless resources. They also hold deep spiritual and recreational significance for communities worldwide.

The Vital Roles of Terrestrial Ecosystems

Life on land is the cornerstone of Earth's natural balance, providing critical services that sustain all forms of life. From stabilizing the climate and purifying water to maintaining soil fertility and supporting cultural and recreational activities, land ecosystems play diverse roles that directly affect human well-being and global biodiversity. Protecting these ecosystems ensures clean air, safe water, abundant food, and spaces for cultural connection, recreation, and mental well-being, making them indispensable for both current and future generations.

  • 🌿 Stores carbon - Helps fight climate change
  • 💧 Purifies water - Keeps water clean and safe
  • 🌾 Protects soil - Prevents erosion, supports farming
  • 🐾 Saves wildlife - Home to diverse species
  • 🌎 Balances climate - Regulates weather patterns
  • 🌸 Holds culture - Preserves traditions and heritage
  • 🧘 Boosts well-being - Nature heals mind and body
  • 🍎 Feeds us - Source of food and raw materials
